Problem Statement
- Hospitals and medical facilities struggle to track high-value, mobile medical equipment (such as C-arm X-ray machines and specialized surgical tools). Because existing digital inventory systems are high-friction, medical professionals rely on a "shadow system" of handwritten, paper sign-out sheets. This analog bottleneck leads to lost assets, delayed procedures, and a massive administrative burden for doctors and nurses who must constantly search for available equipment.


Target Users & Benefits

Who uses it:

- Surgical Coordinators and Floor Nurses
- Hospital Asset Managers
- Physicians and Clinical Staff
  
How it helps:
- Eliminates the need to physically search hospital wings for equipment.
- Digitizes messy handwritten logs instantly without forcing staff to change their workflow.
- Predicts machine availability and creates a real-time "heatmap" of equipment usage.
- Reduces administrative "pajama time" by bridging the gap between paper logs and digital databases.


Solution Overview
- We are creating an AI-powered asset tracking application designed for the fast-paced medical environment. A user simply snaps a photo of a physical equipment sign-out sheet using their mobile device. Our AI processes the image, extracts the handwriting (identifying the user, machine, and location), and instantly updates a live dashboard. By transforming a static piece of paper into real-time data, the system coordinates equipment availability across the hospital floor seamlessly.


Tech Stack

Ollama:
Why: Allows us to run multimodal/vision LLMs locally. This is crucial for securely processing images of sign-out sheets for OCR and entity extraction without sending sensitive hospital data to external APIs.

FastAPI:
Why: Provides a lightweight, high-performance backend to handle image buffer uploads and serve as the asynchronous bridge coordinating the frontend requests with the local AI models.

PostgreSQL:
Why: It securely stores the parsed equipment statuses, current locations, and historical usage logs generated by the AI.

Next.js:
Why: Enables the creation of a clean, responsive mobile web interface for the "Snap & Sync" camera function, alongside a fast, real-time dashboard for staff to view the equipment heatmap.
